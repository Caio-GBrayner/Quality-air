Quality Meaning Recommendation
Good Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk to health. Concentrations of air pollutants meet the World Health Organization (WHO) guidelines. No restrictions for the general population.
Moderate Air quality is acceptable. However, for some pollutants, there might be a moderate health concern for a very small number of people who are unusually sensitive to air pollution. Sensitive groups (children, the elderly, asthmatics, people with respiratory or heart diseases) should consider limiting prolonged or high-intensity outdoor exertion.
Poor Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected. Active children and adults, and people with respiratory diseases such as asthma, should avoid all outdoor exertion; everyone else, especially children, should limit outdoor exertion.
Hazardous Health alert: everyone may experience more serious health effects. There's a risk of premature mortality among the elderly, children, and people with pre-existing conditions. Everyone should avoid all outdoor activities. In some cases, it may be recommended to stay indoors with air purifiers.

Welcome!

This project delves into the analysis and prediction of air quality using the power of data and Artificial Intelligence (AI). Our main goal is to unravel the complex factors that contribute to air pollution, from industrial and vehicle emissions to meteorological and geographic conditions. By collecting and processing comprehensive data on pollutants, traffic, weather patterns, and other relevant variables, we develop advanced AI models. These models are able to identify hidden trends, correlations, and patterns, allowing us to more accurately understand pollution behavior in different scenarios. More than just understanding the past, our project aims to provide a robust and accessible forecasting tool. This tool will allow us to anticipate pollution spikes, identify risk areas, and provide valuable information to decision-makers and the general public. With clear, data-driven projections, we hope to empower individuals and communities to take proactive preventive actions, such as adjusting daily routines, planning outdoor activities, or implementing mitigation measures during critical periods. Ultimately, this work aims to promote greater awareness of the importance of air quality for human health and the environment. By making data and predictions more accessible and understandable, we hope to inspire and facilitate the adoption of habits and policies that contribute to cleaner air and a healthier future for all.

Interpretation:

  • Precision: Of all the predictions for a class, how many were correct.
  • Recall: Dof all the real instances of a class, how many did the model identify?
  • F1-Score: Harmonic mean between precision and recall.
  • support: How many samples were used.
